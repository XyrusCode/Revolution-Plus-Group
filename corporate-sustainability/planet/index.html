Addressing Climate Change

The past two centuries have seen myriad “tipping points” for our planet – climate change, global warming, the changing environment, urbanisation and population growth which are clear indicators that we must find a balance between meeting the demands of our global community and maintaining the ecological equilibrium to sustain our planet.

We continue to address these emergencies through our sustainable business practices, smart integrated townships, and our partnership with the United Nations Sustainable Development Solutions Network (UN-SDSN) as part of our ongoing efforts to build a more progressive and sustainable future for all.

Urban Forestry and Farms

We perform conservation and rehabilitation efforts in our cities as well as urban farming for efficient land use. Urban farms create green spaces, recycle waste, reduce mileage on food transportation, provide employment, prevent erosion and act as a natural carbon sequestration system, thus building resilient cities.

Sunway City Kuala Lumpur

  • We run biodiversity awareness programmes in Sunway Lagoon Theme Park’s petting zoo.
  • We were rated “excellent” by the Department of Wildlife and National Parks for our practices.
  • In 2019, we started the Sunway Seeding Inspiration and Leadership via Knowledge (SILK) corporate initiative, with the objective of teaching the students of SMK Bandar Sunway to create their own urban farms, and transform them into a social enterprise by 2021.
  • We will partner with Thought For Food, an organisation that targets and is working to create a more resilient and inclusive food system by 2050, to raise new urban farmers amongst youth.

Sunway City Ipoh

  • The Lost World of Tambun has signed a memorandum of understanding with the Malacca Zoo to carry out wildlife conservation programmes.
  • We have recently built an approximately 20-acre organic farm within the township. Different types of fruits and vegetables are grown in the farm including durian, dokong, rambutan, lady’s fingers, kangkong, corn, choy sum, cucumber, carrot and purple spinach. The produce is harvested for the farm-to-fork menu at The Banjaran Hotsprings Retreat as well as to be used as feed at our deer farm.

Sunway City Iskandar Puteri

  • We have set up an eco club in Sunway International School to teach young students about nature and environmental awareness. Activities include planting and taking care of trees in the club.

Green Initiatives

We are mindful of the impact of development on our environment, and constantly strive to be more efficient and conscientious in exploring new ways of going green. Here are some of our green initiatives.

Zero Single-Use Plastics

To reduce the consumption of single-use plastics, we practise the Bring Your Own Bottle (BYOB) concept at all internal events, where participants are encouraged to bring their own water bottles, containers and cutlery. In our malls, the Metal Straw Campaign is launched as part of the effort. Shoppers receive a set of metal straw when they spend a minimum of RM100 in two receipts mall-wide. A total of 19,800 plastic bottles were saved (1,800 from the Northern region and 18,000 from the Central region).

World Earth Day 2019

In conjunction with World Earth Day, Sunway Lagoon Theme Park organised a recycling competition in hopes of creating awareness of sustainable development amongst staff. The theme park successfully achieved its goal of collecting 1,000kg of recyclable materials and even surpassed it by 600kg, having collected a total of 1,600kg.

Fabric Recycling

Textile dye and microplastics from synthetic clothing pollute water bodies, thus endangering aquatic life and ultimately human health. In an effort to combat this, Kloth Cares bins are installed across several of our properties. Fabrics that are collected will be donated to charitable organisations, and exported to developing countries, to be reused as industrial wiping cloth or converted into fuel and energy.

Tackling Food Waste

We have invested in a compost machine designed to process up to 500 litres of food waste every day, hence saving 180,000 litres of food waste from landfills every year. Food composting machines installed in Sunway City Kuala Lumpur reduce up to 80% of food waste within 24 hours by transforming it into bio-organic compost.

In Menara Sunway, food waste is stored in a refuse chamber and sent to a site to be ingested by Black Soldier Fly (BSF) larvae and earthworms to form fertiliser. The BSF larvae and earthworms are fed to poultry farms and fisheries, while the organic fertiliser is used to nourish vegetable crops.

Repurposing Used Soap

The Soapful initiative is a collaboration between Sunway Hotels & Resorts, Ecolab Sdn Bhd, Kinder Soaps Sdn Bhd and Selangor Youth Community (SAY) aimed at repurposing used soap bars collected from the hotels. They are donated to communities in need to raise awareness of self-hygiene.

Repurposing Used Cooking Oil

Several of our hotels and theme park actively repurpose used cooking oil into biodiesel to minimise cooking oil waste. In partnership with FatHopes Energy, this initiative helps prevent drains from getting clogged and reduces reliance on fossil fuels, thus eliminating GHG emissions.

More than 50% of the used cooking oil generated by the Group’s six participating hotels is collected by FatHopes Energy weekly to be converted into biodiesel.

Trash For Tree

Sunway Hotel Hanoi organised a “Trash for Tree” project, in which 300kg of papers and 200 units of used batteries are collected in exchange for six potted plants with the Green Life Project.

Efficient Resource Management

Our business, as well as the use of our products and services depend on and impact the natural environment in a variety of ways. We leverage on our Build-Own-Operate business model to create future-proof, climate-resilient communities through the entire value chain of our townships’ and assets’ lifecycle.

Efficient Land Use and Construction Management

  • While acquiring new land bank, we focus on lands that are suitable for compact and mixed developments in high-density urban areas to mitigate urban sprawl. Transit-oriented, walkable developments promote car-lite urban mobility and encourage the use of public transportation. We plan for mixed developments which reduce distances between places where people live, learn, work and play to mitigate GHG emissions. Six of our largest mixed developments are built on brownfield sites.
  • In pursuit of green construction, we expand our virtual design construction capabilities to 7D, adopt green construction methods such as IBS and system formwork as well as source for green building materials such as AAC blocks and eco pavements.

Efficient Energy Management

  • In 2019, we carried out retrofit projects in five of our malls, seven hotels, five office towers and both theme parks to progressively decrease our Building Energy Intensity.
  • We completed the installation of solar PV panels at Monash University, Sunway University and Sunway Ecowalk, together with assets owned by Sunway Paving Solutions and Sunway Big Box Retail Park which will produce 2.56 GWh of electricity.

Efficient Water Management

  • We invested RM18 million in research and installation of water treatment plant in Sunway City Kuala Lumpur and Sunway South Quay which can supply up to 8,400 m3 water daily. Measuring against WHO’s recommendation of 165 litres of domestic water consumption per person daily, this amount of water is sufficient to be supplied to 50,909 individuals per day.
  • Rainwater is harvested for non-portable use.
  • Recycled water and drought-resistant plants are used at Menara Sunway and The Pinnacle.
